Kollam: World's Cashew Capital

A famous port of international spice trade in ancient India, the present day Kollam is famous for its varied modern industries including cashew nut processing and research. Its association with the renowned Ashtamudi Lake makes it the gateway to the magnificent backwaters of Kerala. As an old seaport town on the Malabar Coast, Kollam had a sustained commercial reputation from the days of the Phoenicians and the Romans.

Places to visit

Ashtamudi Lake

The second largest lake in Kerala, is surrounded by lush green trees and swaying coconut palms. This lake is known for its panoramic beauty and eight channels that connects to one lake. It is because of its eight 'arms' or channels, that the lake is named Ashtamudi.

Jatayu’s Earth Centre

The Jatayu Earth’s Center hosts the world’s largest bird sculpture. You can reach the hilltop using a cable car. This is a perfect weekend getaway and offers breathtaking views of nature.

Kollam Adventure Park

An urban park in the core Kollam city of Kerala state. It is the main centre of recreational activities in Kollam city. The park is home to many endangered species of trees. Thick mangroves that grow on the shore of Ashtamudi Lake along the side of the Adventure Park is another significant attraction in this spot.
