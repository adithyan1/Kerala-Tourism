Alappuzha: Backwaters and Houseboats

Reknowned as the Venice of the East, Alappuzha has an important place in the maritime history of Kerala. Today, it is famous for its boat races, backwater holidays, beaches, marine products and coir industry. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, held on the Punnamada Lake, near Alappuzha, is the most popular and competitive boat races in India

Places to visit

Alappey Backwaters

The most popular backwater destinations in Kerala, Alleppey is one such destination that must not be missed! Leisure seekers, honeymooners, adventurers, or visitors from any walk of life can visit here and create pleasant memories with their friends and families.

Ambalapuzha Sree Krishna Temple

A Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna situated in Alappuzha district. Built-in traditional Kerala style architectural pattern, the temple is famous for its delicious rice pudding prepared in sweet milk, popularly known as Pal Payasam.

Alappey Beach

Come experience the sea met by backwaters, lagoons and rivers. The beach is crowned with a 137-year-old long pier that stretches out into the city.
