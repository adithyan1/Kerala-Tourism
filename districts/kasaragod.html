Kasaragod: The Land of Gods

Kasaragod is known as the land of gods, forts, rivers, hills and beautiful beaches. Kasaragod displays a variety of styles in temple architecture - the unique style of the Madhur Mahaganapathy Temple, the typical Kerala style of the historic Malik Deenar Great Juma Masjid and so on speak volumes about the rich cultural heritage of the region. The district is the northernmost district of the State of Kerala.

Places to visit

Chandragiri Fort

This is a fort built in the 17th century. Those visiting this serene location will be rewarded with a panoramic view of the lush landscape below. This large squarish fort is 150 feet above sea level and occupies an area of about seven acres adjacent to Chandragiri river.

Ranipuram

Ranipuram boasts of some of the best trekking trails in the area. Situated 750 m above sea level, this is a must-visit for all nature enthusiasts.

Ananthapura Lake Temple

This is the only lake temple in the state and is almost 1000 years old. A vegetarian crocodile named Babia is another attraction of this temple. There are no restrictions to visit this temple and it is open to everybody irrespective of religion, caste or creed.
