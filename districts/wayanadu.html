Wayanadu: Green Paradise

The name Wayanad is derived from Vayal Nadu which means the land of paddy fields. It is a picturesque plateau famous for its large amount of camping and trekking trails, breathtaking waterfalls, caves, and wildlife. This area has been a tourist favourite over the years. A wide range of exotic products including spices, coffee, tea, bamboo products, honey and herbal plants are available here.

Places to visit

Meenmutty Waterfalls

Meenmutty Falls gives a glimpse of natural beauty surrounded by the mountain ranges of Western Ghats. The waterfall is located upriver the Neyyar reservoir area and travellers, have to trek through the dense forests to reach the falls. Adventurous minds are welcomed from here to an off-route trekking to top of Meenmutty Falls.

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a natural habitat of many rare and endangered flora and fauna and a wonderful getaway for wildlife lovers. The two most important areas in Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary are Muthanga and Tholpetty. The sanctuary offers an excellent opportunity to see elephant herds, deer, langurs and tigers roam freely. Tourists here can also indulge in bird watching or go trekking in the forest. This biodiversity hotspot is a great place to see in Wayanad.

Edakkal Caves

A trip to Edakkal Caves is like a journey into a forgotten past. They have provided historians with great information regarding the lives and habits of the ancestors here. The caves are two natural rock formations believed to have been formed by a large split in a huge rock. The carvings inside are extremely beautiful. A trek up the majestic Ambukuthi Hills is required to reach these caves. The aroma of coffee stays with you the entire way. It is truly a surreal experience to step into the palms of history.
