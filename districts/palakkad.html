Palakkad: Granary of Kerala

Palakkad is a distinctive place to visit and offers a contrasting view to most of Kerala dotted with coastlines and coconut groves. This place has an abundance of giant Palmyra groves. Apart from being known as the ‘Gateway to Kerala’, Palakkad is a major granary to the state.

Places to visit

Palakkad Fort

a beautifully maintained edifice and an important military base in the past. It is among the best preserved forts in South India. The thick laterite walls are impressive and people frequent the spot for relaxing walks and jogs. A large ground that lies between the Fort and the Palakkad town hall is an important location for public meetings and important functions. Today it is a protected monument under the Archaeological Survey of India. It is a preferred picnic spot for everyone visiting Palakkad.

Malampuzha Dam

One of the largest dams and reservoirs in Kerala. Malampuzha Garden in Palakkad district is the only rock-cut garden in South India. The flowering beds, fountains and rose gardens along with an aerial ropeway make it a place that instantly soothes your soul.

Silent Valley National Park

This reserve of tropical rainforests is a must visit. The diversity in flora and fauna is breath-taking. The park offers several eco-tourism packages for travellers like trekking and camping.
